# Letterfest Hackathon 2026

## Build something a customer could use _next month_.

|              |                                      |
| ------------ | ------------------------------------ |
| **When**     | Wednesday evening → Friday lunchtime |
| **Format**   | Small teams, one prototype each      |
| **Tools**    | Cursor or Claude Code                |
| **Demo day** | Friday lunchtime — show, don't tell  |

## The brief

Letterfest makes personalised products that people love. But we know there are experiences, tools, and product ideas we haven't built yet — things that could delight customers, open new channels, or make something magical out of the tech that's now available to us.

**Your challenge:** identify **one idea** that makes Letterfest's products more **valuable**, more **accessible**, or more **magical** — and build a **working prototype** in a day.

The best ideas will be:

- **Customer-facing** — something a real buyer, gifter, or partner would interact with
- **Buildable** — a functional prototype in ~8 hours, not a slide deck
- **Commercially sharp** — you can explain who'd pay for it and why

## Where to look for ideas

These are starting points to spark thinking — not to constrain it.

### New digital add-ons

Things that increase order value or make the product feel more premium. Audiobooks narrated in the customer's own voice. AR previews of memorial stones placed in the garden. Video message cards with QR codes. A "story behind your gift" shareable page for every order.

### New audiences

Vertical microsites or tools aimed at a specific buyer. Estate agents buying house portraits as welcome gifts for buyers. Corporate gifting portals with bulk personalisation. HR teams ordering 50 personalised books for new starters.

### New buying experiences

Making it easier, faster, or more fun to buy. An AI gift concierge that asks a few questions and recommends the right product. "Your child as the hero" — AI-generated personalised children's books. A design-your-own preview tool for any product line.

### Something we haven't thought of

The best idea probably isn't on this list.
